What is bodyweight workout?

Bodyweight workouts leverage the body's weight as resistance, without needing equipment. Movements such as squats and push-ups enhance strength, flexibility, and endurance. They're accessible, adaptable to all fitness levels, and offer a cost-effective way to improve overall fitness. Their versatility allows for adjustments in intensity, making them suitable for beginners to advanced athletes. Integrating these exercises into routines can lead to significant improvements in physical health and well-being. Whether at home, outdoors, or while traveling, bodyweight workouts provide a convenient and efficient means of staying fit. Their simplicity and effectiveness make them a popular choice among individuals looking to achieve and maintain their fitness goals without the constraints of a gym or specialized equipment.

What type of training should I do to lose weight

To lose weight effectively, a combination of cardio and strength training is recommended. Cardio exercises, such as running, cycling, or swimming, help burn calories and improve cardiovascular health. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity or 75 minutes of high-intensity cardio per week. Strength training, involving weight lifting or body-weight exercises like squats and push-ups, helps build muscle, which increases your resting metabolic rate, enabling you to burn more calories even at rest. Incorporating high-intensity interval training (HIIT) can also be highly effective, as it combines intense bursts of activity with short rest periods, maximizing calorie burn in a shorter time. Consistency, coupled with a balanced diet, is key to successful weight loss.
